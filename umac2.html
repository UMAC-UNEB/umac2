The Board

Governance and oversight

Prof. Ainembabazi Emma Paula

Chairperson

Hello and welcome to UMAC. The Uganda Marking Association Centre (UMAC) was initially established by an Act of Parliament in 1983, Cap 137 of the Laws of Uganda. The Board is a body corporate mandated to conduct and manage examinations in Uganda.

Statutory Functions of UMAC

  1. Conduct primary, secondary, and such other examinations within Uganda as it may consider desirable in the public interest;
  2. Publish past examination papers;
  3. Award certificates to successful candidates in such examinations;
  4. Invite anybody or bodies outside Uganda, as it may think fit, and to jointly conduct academic and other examinations;
  5. Award acceptable certificates to successful candidates jointly with the invited bodies;
  6. Invite other bodies, as it may think fit, to conduct examinations and award acceptable certificates or diplomas;
  7. Advise anybody or bodies so invited under paragraph (f) upon the adoption of examinations necessary for the requirements of Uganda and to assist any such body or bodies to conduct such examinations;
  8. Make arrangements for the conduct of research and development of examination systems;
  9. Make rules regulating the conduct of examinations and for all purposes incidental thereto.

Programs undertaken by UMAC

  • Assessment and Certification of Primary Leaving Examination
  • Assessment and Certification of Uganda Certificate of Education
  • Assessment and Certification of Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education
  • Equating of Results of Foreign Qualifications (PLE, UCE & UACE)
  • Verification of results for PLE, UCE and UACE
  • National Assessment of Progress in Education (P.3, P.6, and S.2)
  • Research geared to improvement in the quality of assessment and certification

The UMAC Examination Cycle

  • Registration of candidates
  • Accreditation of Examination Centers
  • Identifying and training of new Examiners
  • Training of new examiners
  • Preparation of question papers (setting, moderation, compilation, typesetting, proofing and banking)
  • Printing and packing done in-house by staff resident in the Printery hostel under strict security measures
  • Stakeholder engagements (field staff – Inspectors, Area and Centre Supervisors and personnel from Security agencies)
  • Field Conduct of Examinations
  • Marking of examination scripts
  • Release of results and certification

Registration of Candidates

The e-registration process is designed to be simple and requires minimal computer skills. The system allows centers to register candidates from a location without internet connectivity and then send data to UMAC from a location where internet services are available.

Guidelines for Registration of Candidates

This process involves the use of electrical methods to carry out registration of candidates and these include the following:

  • Window-based desktop application.
  • On-line registration.
  • Ensure all candidate details are captured correctly and accurately before final submission through the web portal.

As the first step, the Association has designed and developed a window-based desktop system for electronically registering UCE and UACE candidates.

MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS

  • Digital photographs of Candidates
  • Computer with Windows Operating System
  • Internet Connection
  • A trained person to do e-Registration
Important Notice: UMAC Normal Registration for 2026 has been extended. Ensure all candidate data is uploaded by 20th August, 2026.

Assessment of Candidates

Assessment Mandate

The Uganda Marking Association Centre (UMAC) serves as the primary assessment body for national examinations in Uganda. The association utilizes a standardized system of examination formats and grading to evaluate candidate performance across various academic levels.

National Examinations Levels

  • Primary Leaving Examination (PLE)
  • Uganda Certificate of Education (UCE)
  • Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education (UACE)

Candidate information and Grading

The assessment process includes the capture of candidate information to ensure accurate recording of results. This information is processed through established examination formats to ensure fair and consistent grading for all candidates sitting for the national examinations.

Awarding of Certificates

Policy on Awarding Certificates

UMAC awards certificates to successful candidates according to the prevailing policies. These certificates are the official recognition of a candidate's achievement in the national examinations conducted by the Association.

Levels of Certification

  • Primary Leaving Examination (PLE)
  • Uganda Certificate of Education (UCE)
  • Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education (UACE)

Historical Background of Certification

Initially, certification was conducted under the East African Examinations Council (EAEC), which issued the East African Certificate. Following the dissolution of the EAEC, Uganda started conducting its own examinations, and the certificates were renamed to current UCE and UACE.

Important Notice: UMAC does not issue duplicate certificates. A letter of verification of results is issued to serve the concerned candidate upon request.

Equating of foreign results

Service Description

The Uganda Marking Association Centre (UMAC) provides an equating service for individuals who hold academic qualification documents obtained from foreign countries. This service is essential for those who wish to use their foreign qualifications for further education or employment within Uganda.

Standardization of Results

The Association evaluates foreign qualification documents and equates such results to the equivalent of UMAC standards. This ensures that foreign academic achievements are accurately aligned within the Ugandan education system.

APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS

Applicants are generally required to present their original qualification documents for verification during the equating process to ensure the authenticity and accuracy of the equivalent standards being awarded.

Where to process from:

While some aspects of data management are handled via the portal, physical processing for specialised letters and certifications is typically coordinated through the Ntinda office block.

Research and Publications

Educational Assessment Research

As the national assessment body, the Association conducts extensive research to improve the quality and integrity of national examinations. This research is used to evaluate candidate performance trends and to inform policy decisions regarding the education system in Uganda.

Key Research Focus

  • Candidate Performance Analysis: Detailed statistical breakdowns of PLE, UCE, and UACE results to identify learning gaps.
  • Examination Formats: Continuous evaluation of inherited and modern grading systems to ensure they meet national standards.
  • Feedback Mechanisms: Gathering data from various examination centres to improve service delivery and exam security.

Available Publications

  • Examination Reports: Annual reports providing feedback on candidate performance in specific subjects at PLE, UCE, and UACE levels.
  • Statistical Bulletins: Comprehensive data sets regarding registration and results across all regions.
  • Newsletters and Journals: Periodic updates on Association activities, policy changes, and educational innovations.

Validation of Exam Centres

Overview of Centre Validation

Validation of Exam Centres is a critical process conducted by the Uganda Marking Association Centre (UMAC) to ensure that all schools and institutions hosting national examinations (PLE, UCE, and UACE) meet the required infrastructure and technical standards.

Minimum Requirements for Validation

  • Hardware: A computer system running a modern Windows Operating System.
  • Personnel: At least one trained person capable of performing e-Registration and managing candidate data.
  • Data Handling: Facilities to capture or store digital photographs of candidates.
  • Connectivity: An internet connection for uploading data to the UMAC portal.

Support for Emerging Centres

  • Teaming: Centres may meet the minimum requirements by teaming with other Schools/Centres in their neighborhood to share ICT facilities.
  • Hiring: Centres are permitted to hire the necessary equipment for one-time use during the registration and examination periods.
  • Shared Infrastructure: A single computer can be used to register candidates from several different centres if needed.

Verification of Results

Policy on Duplicate Certificates

UMAC does not issue duplicate certificates. In the event of loss or damage to result slips and/or certificates, a Letter of Verification of Results (LVR) is issued to serve the concerned candidate.

Requirements for Individuals

  • Bring a letter from the school or centre of sitting.
  • Appear in person. (Candidates outside the country should send a copy of their identity document and an authorizing letter for a representative).
  • Bring a letter from the Police in case of loss or damage of a slip or certificate.
  • Present a copy of a valid identity card.
  • Pay processing fees as determined by the Board.

Requirements for Institutions and Agencies

  • A letter of request addressed to the Executive Secretary, UMAC.
  • A typed list of persons whose results are to be verified, indicating the level of examination (PLE, UCE, or UACE), year of sitting, and correct index number.
  • Photocopies of the result documents to be verified.
  • Payment against an invoice issued by the Association.

Processing Fees and Location

  • Ordinary Letter: 40,000 Shillings.
  • Hard Card (Certificate Substitute): 80,000 Shillings.

The processing of Letters of Verification of Results is done at the Ntinda office block. You may also apply online through the portal.
