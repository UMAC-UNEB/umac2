Validation of Exam Centres

Overview of Centre Validation

Validation of Exam Centres is a critical process conducted by the Uganda Marking Association Centre (UMAC) to ensure that all schools and institutions hosting national examinations (PLE, UCE, and UACE) meet the required infrastructure and technical standards.

This process ensures that a centre is capable of handling candidate data securely and conducting examinations with the necessary integrity.

Minimum Requirements for Validation

For a School/Centre to be validated, especially for the purpose of the e-Registration programme, they should possess the following:

  • Hardware: A computer system running a modern Windows Operating System.
  • Personnel: At least one trained person capable of performing e-Registration and managing candidate data.
  • Data Handling: Facilities to capture or store digital photographs of candidates.
  • Connectivity: An internet connection for uploading data to the UMAC portal. (In cases where internet is absent, centres must have an export facility to move data to an Excel workbook for uploading from a location with internet access).

Support for Emerging Centres

UMAC recognizes that some new or smaller centres may not yet have full computer facilities. To ensure validation is still possible, the Association allows for the following:

  • Teaming: Centres may meet the minimum requirements by teaming with other Schools/Centres in their neighborhood to share ICT facilities.
  • Hiring: Centres are permitted to hire the necessary equipment for one-time use during the registration and examination periods.
  • Shared Infrastructure: A single computer can be used to register candidates from several different centres if needed.

Operational Standards

Once a centre is validated, it is responsible for the accuracy of the data submitted. All information entered into the provided templates must be uploaded to the UMAC portal for thorough verification and confirmation of accuracy. Validated centres must also ensure that institution choices are submitted at the time of candidate registration.
